Journey Through Time

2018

Combat Robotics Geeks

Met in the official robotics club of VIT Vellore, Hriteek and Shivanshu clicked instantly.
The mechanical-engineer and electronics-engineer duo worked on lots of projects throughout and spent 4 years building combat robots together.
Their shared problem-solving mindset and passion for technology and engineering allowed them to work on a lot of challenges together.

2021

E-SkateBoard

VIT being a huge campus, Hriteek and Shivanshu solved their own campus commute problem by building personal light electric vehicles like electric skateboards and scooters. This is precisely when they fell in love with Electric Vehicles (EVs).




Movio & Micro-Mobility

While zooming through the campus on their sleek electric skateboards, Hriteek and Shivanshu caught the attention of VIT-TBI. The incubator nurtured their project and helped them develop it into a business model.
Soon, they registered the company 'Movio Technologies Private Limited' and secured a funding of 2.2 lakh INR to pursue their venture. They began exploring the realm of micromobility in Bangalore using personal light electric vehicles (PLEVs) and created impressive products. However, through conversations with EV riders, they quickly realized that the major issue with electric vehicles wasn't the vehicles themselves, but the batteries.

2022

Enter, Vishrut

Hriteek and Shivanshu saw the need for someone with strong software development skills on their team.
Shivanshu reconnected with his old friend from Kota, Vishrut Vatsa, who was then at Boeing R&D and had notable experience in the software industry, including providing software to IISc Bangalore and interning at Apple. Recognizing his exceptional skills and precision, Shivanshu invited him to join as a co-founder.
Vishrut exited Boeing to embark on the 0 to 1 journey with Movio.

Government Grant

Their full-stack prototypes & made-in-India approach with impressive UI/UX soon caught the attention of incubators like IIT Roorkee and VIT-TBI. Through the Nidhi Prayas Scheme and MeitY TIDE 2.0 scale-up scheme, they secured a total funding of 40 lakh INR. This was the turning point when the team decided to pursue entrepreneurship full-time and got committed to solving the EV-battery problem.

2023

Enter, Paridhi

The team strategically relocated to Delhi, attracted by its supportive EV policies and conducive environment for hardware businesses. Then the team made the final addition to its founding team, Paridhi.
Paridhi and Shivanshu, being cousins, grew up together. Shivanshu knew Paridhi always was interested in business and entrepreneurship, her background in economics and human resource management made her an ideal fit for complementing the tech-focused founders. Upon relocating to Delhi, their immediate priority was to build a team, a task that would have been significantly challenging without Paridhi's expertise and contributions.

Team Building

Movio founders built a lean team of passionate engineers and BDs. Operating initially out of a small office in Gurgaon, they focused on hardware prototyping and developing a scalable business model.
The tech team worked tirelessly, while the business development team engaged with customers to identify their pain points.
Following the lean startup methodology, they built, learned, iterated, and repeated the process to refine their products and approach.

Prototyping Stage

It took them 4 prototypes of the battery pack and 2 iterations of the swap hub to achieve their initial goal: creating a minimum viable product (MVP). They tested the entire technology stack with a small group of users to understand what worked and what didn't. Before long, they had a fully functional prototype and a simple MVP ready to be launched.

MVP

They ran the MVP with high-intent EV riders who used their electric vehicles throughout the day to gather extensive data and validations. The MVP was tested across various use cases, from Swiggy delivery executives to heavy-cargo logistics. They also tested the flexibility of home charging combined with battery swapping, confirming that customers appreciate the convenience of charging at home while having the option to swap batteries when needed. The MVP provided the team with valuable insights.

GridXenergy Re-branding

Based on insights from the MVP and interactions with industry experts, the team realized significant changes were necessary.
Firstly, they decided to move away from limiting their solutions to low-speed EVs, recognizing the market shift towards high-speed EVs and the rising demand for personal mobility solutions. With these learnings, Movio was rebranded as GridXenergy. The battery packs were enhanced to be more powerful, with a focus on meticulous attention to detail. UI/UX improvements were implemented specifically to appeal to personal mobility users, enhancing aesthetics, design, and portability as well.

2024

Product Showcasing

Soon, the industrial design prototypes of GridX were completed, and the team began showcasing their products at various events and platforms. They presented their innovations at venues ranging from the Uttarakhand Investor Summit to colleges in Pune, demonstrating their products to diverse audiences.

SUN Mobility

A pivotal moment occurred when Shivanshu cold-texted Chetan Maini from SUN Mobility, leading the GridX team to meet the "Godfather" of India's EV industry. This opportunity enabled them to visit SUN Mobility's manufacturing plant, pitch their B2C brand concept, and showcase their technology to seasoned engineers. During this visit, they found a mentor in Mr. Anand Ganapathy Chennira, SUN's Chief Strategy Officer, who has provided invaluable guidance since December 2023. This experience was transformative for team GridX, and they cherished every aspect of it as a significant learning opportunity.

Validations & Early Traction

Finally, GridXenergy successfully demonstrated its full-stack technology with an EV OEM, GT Force, securing the first Letter of Intent (LOI) for future collaboration. Following this milestone, GridXenergy also received a pre-order of 100 vehicles from a respected fleet owner in Delhi NCR, solely based on the demonstration of their functional prototypes. This validation underscored the market interest in their product. Currently, the GridXenergy team is focused on executing their go-to-market strategy with renewed approach.

Rigorous Testing

GridXenergy conducted rigorous field testing with a dedicated EV rider to ensure its full-stack system was battle-tested in real-world conditions. The rider extensively used the PowerPod and the app, pushing the system to its limits through daily operations, long routes, and heavy usage. Performance data and rider feedback enabled the team to refine system stability, optimize the app, and validate the technology's reliability under real conditions, proving it is ready for the road.

2025

First Customer

GridXenergy partnered with a local dealership, where the team met a woman frustrated by battery failures and high replacement costs. Initially skeptical of GridX's Battery-as-a-Service model, she insisted, "Nahi nahi, humein wahi wali battery chahiye." Undeterred, the team patiently explained the benefits of zero upfront cost, no maintenance, and pay-per-use. Skepticism turned to curiosity, then trust. In the end, she agreed to retrofit her vehicle. This wasn't just a sale, it was proof that GridXenergy is solving real problems and making a difference.
